"Hence men undertake wide-ranging travel, and wander over remote shores, and their fickleness, always discontented with the present, gives proof of itself now on land and now on sea.
They undertake one journey after another and change spectacle for spectacle.
They began to be sick of life and the world itself, and [think]:

“How long shall I endure the same things?”

"On Tranquility" Seneca
